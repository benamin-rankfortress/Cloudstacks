Recognizing When You Need Marriage Counseling - Recognizing the signs of a relationship that may require marriage counseling is an important first step towards betterment. Here are some common situations that might indicate you need professional intervention:

Frequent Conflicts and Arguments - All couples fight, but it’s not the frequency of arguments that’s concerning, but the way they’re handled. If your arguments often involve hurtful words, unkind jabs, or even name-calling, it’s time to consider marriage counseling. As a professional therapist, we can help break these destructive cycles and teach you healthier ways of handling conflict.

Emotional Distance or Lack of Intimacy - Feeling emotionally detached or experiencing a lack of intimacy in your relationship can be a sign that you need help. If you’re frequently feeling lonely or distant from your partner, it’s crucial to address these issues before they lead to more significant problems. We can help you navigate these feelings and rebuild emotional closeness in your relationship.

Infidelity and Trust Issues - Infidelity can be a devastating blow to any marriage. But remember, it’s not just physical — even emotional betrayals or secretiveness can cause damage. If you or your partner have had an affair or there are ongoing trust issues, seek support from a trained professional. We can guide you in rebuilding trust and healing from the pain caused by infidelity.

Major Life Transitions - Significant life changes, like a career shift, the birth of a child, or retirement, can put immense stress on a relationship. If you’re struggling to navigate these transitions together, marriage counseling can provide the support you need. We can help you communicate better, manage your stress, and adapt to new roles and routines.

Parenting Disagreements - Differing parenting styles can create conflicts and lead to discord. Whether it’s about discipline methods, educational choices, or setting boundaries, disagreements on parenting can strain your relationship. In such cases, counseling can be instrumental in finding common ground and developing a unified approach to parenting.

The Role of Individual Therapy in Marriage Counseling. Marriage counseling is not a one-size-fits-all process. It’s a personalized journey that accommodates the unique needs of each partner. Sometimes, this may require individual therapy sessions. Let’s delve into why and when individual therapy can be a vital part of marriage counseling.

When One Partner is Resistant to Therapy - It’s natural for one partner to feel hesitant or resistant to marriage counseling. However, this shouldn’t be a roadblock on your journey towards healing. At Peace of Mind Wellness & Family Counseling, we believe that therapy can still be effective even if one partner initially shows reluctance.

As highlighted by Psychology Today, marriage counseling can work if one partner convinces the other to participate. The hesitant partner may start to see the value of therapy once the process begins and they experience the positive impact it can have on their relationship.

In such cases, individual therapy can be a useful stepping stone. It allows the reluctant partner to explore their feelings about the relationship and the counseling process, potentially paving the way for them to engage in couples therapy.

Addressing Individual Issues that Affect the Relationship. Sometimes, individual issues can seep into the relationship, causing discord and misunderstanding. These problems could range from mental health struggles to past trauma and loss. As part of our marriage counseling services, we offer individual therapy to address these personal issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

I am interested in telehealth; how do I determine if it is an option for me?

 

We encourage you to discuss your interest in telehealth with your clinician during your next appointment to determine if this might be a fit for you. Unless you are linked to a Telehealth exclusive site, currently telehealth is offered only for follow-up appointments, not first-time patients. If you do not currently have an appointment scheduled, please contact us.

What if my provider doesn’t offer telehealth?

 

If you do not see your provider listed as an available Telehealth participant, please to find out more information.

Will my insurance cover telehealth visits?

 

Most commercial insurances do cover telehealth visits, but coverage can vary by insurance carrier. We strongly recommend that you contact your insurance carrier prior to scheduling a telehealth visit, to inquire if telehealth is indeed a covered benefit for your plan.

Are telehealth video appointments HIPAA Complaint?

 

Peace of Mind Wellness and Family Counseling, Inc. ensures the interaction between the patient and clinician is completely secure and HIPAA compliant. The teleconferencing software encrypts all forms of data, including video and audio.

How To Cancel My Appointment

 

Please call your location to cancel an appointment or select “Cancel” on the appointment reminder you receive to your email. This must be done at least 48 hours in advance or your cancellation can not be received.
