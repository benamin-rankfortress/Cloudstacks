How To Choose the Right Basement Finishing Contractor

Basement Remodeling Contractor

Once you're done firming out your budget for your basement remodeling project, it's time to look for the right contractor to do the job.

Basement finishing is considered a huge project and an investment. That being said, hiring a basement finishing contractor should be well thought out and done with much research. Not only are you trusting this person or company with the property of your family, but also with a very important investment in your life. We've listed some of the most important questions to ask any potential basement finishing contractor before you make your final decision.

What's your contracting company's specialty or focus?

There are many different types of contractors out there. Some might specialize in electrical work, while others might focus on plumbing or HVAC. It's important to find a contractor that specializes in all aspects of basement finishing.​

Are you a licensed contractor?

In most states, contractors are required to be licensed in order to do work on your home. This is for your protection and peace of mind. It is just one step in ensuring that your project doesn't go awry and that you are covered from every angle.

Do you have insurance as a general contractor?

There's always a possibility that something can go wrong while your contractors are doing the basement finishing. Having an insured contractor will protect you in case of an accident or damage to your home. At least have a contract that covers the value of your home against any unforeseen accidents or damage.

How many projects have you finished like mine?

You don't want to be the first time your contractor is doing a job like yours. They should have plenty of experience with projects similar to yours so that you know they can handle it. 5 years of experience is a good indicator that they are knowledgeable and experienced enough to do a job, but there's no set amount of time that determines the skill level someone has. View their previous work, and ask questions.

Can you show me your past work?

Ask for a reference. Ask for the phone number of their previous clients and give them a call. Most importantly, ask to see pictures of their previous work. This will give you the best idea of what to expect from them.

Will you take care of pulling permits?

There's a good chance you'll need a permit for your basement finishing project. This is especially true if you're doing any major structural changes, like adding walls, adjusting the foundation, or changing the layout of the space. Make sure your contractor is willing to take care of the permit process for you. ​

What type of warranty or guarantee do you offer?

You should always get some type of warranty or guarantee from your contractor. This will protect you in case anything goes wrong with the work after it's finished. Most contractors will offer a warranty on their work. Some even offer a limited lifetime warranty, so be sure to ask.

Do you have design options?

Some basement finishing contractors have their own design team or professional designers they work with on a regular basis. This can be a great option if you're not sure what you want or need help making decisions. If you're not sure what you want your basement to look like, ask your contractor if they offer design services. Many contractors will have a few different design options that you can choose from. This can take the guesswork out of designing your basement. ​

Basement finishing ideas

In most cases, the refinished basement is not counted as the livable square footage of your house. It is often used as storage space, hobby rooms, entertainment rooms, or an extra bedroom. However, refinishing your basement gives you an advantage when you plan to sell your house as potential homebuyers will highly consider a house with a finished basement.

​Here are some of the basement finishing ideas you can consider:

Install a Bar - If you are a fan of hosting parties or get-togethers, then you might want to install a wet bar in your basement. It will be a great place to hang out with your friends and have some fun. You don't need a lot of space to install a wet bar—a small countertop would suffice. Pay attention to the ceiling height as well so you can have enough clearance for your bar stools.

Ceiling Insulation - R-19 fiberglass batts are usually installed between the floor joists to insulate the basement ceiling. If you are planning to put up a home theater, then having your ceiling insulated can help to reduce the noise from the basement. In addition, an insulated ceiling can also help to keep your basement warm in winter and cool in summer.
