SPRING CLEANING FOR YOUR WELL IN NORTHERN VIRGINIA - With spring just around the corner, it’s important to prepare your well so that it has the longest lifespan possible and continues to offer fresh and healthy water for your home. Just as you are likely cleaning out the interior of your home for spring, you should do the same for your well.

To learn about what spring well cleaning and maintenance looks like, read below.

CHECK YOUR WELL - At the end of winter, you want to perform your own visual inspection of your well system for damage. Especially if you have well problems, such as low water pressure, make sure to diligently inspect all parts of the well. You never know if snow or ice has damaged it over the last couple of months.

You will want to look for cracks in the casing, exposed wires, pipe fizzers, tilted well caps, and water filters. At this point, you might need to change the treatment filters as well.

FLUSH AND REFILL THE SYSTEM - If you drained and did not use the well system over the winter, you will need to turn on the breaker and allow every faucet to run in the home. This will flush out the system. Allow the water to run for at least five minutes and then check for any new leaks. You can skip this step if you have been using your well system.

SCHEDULE A MAINTENANCE APPOINTMENT - Once you have finished inspecting your well system, it’s a good idea to schedule a maintenance appointment from a water well pump service near me. A well professional will be able to double-check your work and fix any problems that may arise.

SIGNS OF WELL PUMP FAILURE: RECOGNIZING THE WARNING SIGNALS - A reliable water supply in our homes cannot be overstated. Many households in rural areas depend on well water systems to meet their daily water needs. Well, pumps are crucial in ensuring a steady and consistent water supply. However, like all mechanical systems, well pumps can experience issues and, if not addressed promptly, can lead to significant disruptions in water availability.

In this blog, we will explore the signs of well pump failure and why it’s essential to recognize these warning signals.

UNDERSTANDING WELL PUMP FUNCTIONALITY - Before we delve into the signs of well pump failure, it’s essential to have a basic understanding of how well pumps function and their role in providing water to households.

Well pumps are the heart of well water systems. They are responsible for drawing water from the well and delivering it to your home’s plumbing system. There are different types of well pumps, including submersible and jet pumps, each with unique features and functions.

Submersible pumps are typically placed deep within the well and are designed to push water to the surface. Jet pumps are installed above ground and use suction to bring water up from the well. Understanding the type of pump in your system is crucial for troubleshooting and maintenance.

KEY SIGNS OF WELL PUMP FAILURE - Now that we have a basic understanding of how well pumps work let’s explore the signs that indicate well pump failure.

LOW WATER PRESSURE - Low water pressure is one of the most common and easily recognizable signs of well pump failure. It indicates that the well pump needs help to maintain the water pressure needed for smooth water flow throughout your home.

To understand the significance of low water pressure, it’s essential to know that well pumps pressurize water from the well and deliver it to your home’s plumbing system. When a well pump functions correctly, it maintains consistent water pressure, ensuring water flows from your faucets, showerheads, and appliances with adequate force. However, if the pump fails or malfunctions, it may struggle to maintain this pressure, resulting in a noticeable drop in water pressure.
