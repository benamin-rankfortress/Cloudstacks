2. Enhanced Interpersonal Relationships

Effective communication lies at the heart of healthy relationships, and mental health counseling provides a platform for individuals to hone their interpersonal skills. By learning to express themselves authentically and assertively, individuals can foster deeper connections with loved ones, colleagues, and friends. Moreover, counseling equips individuals with conflict resolution techniques, enabling them to navigate disagreements and misunderstandings constructively. As a result, relationships are strengthened, and individuals experience a heightened sense of satisfaction and fulfillment in their social interactions.

3. Increased Self-esteem and Confidence

Self-doubt and insecurity can hold individuals back from realizing their full potential. Through the process of counseling, individuals confront these internal barriers, uncovering their inherent worth and capabilities. As they work towards personal growth and achievement, they set and achieve meaningful goals, bolstering their self-esteem and confidence. Armed with a positive self-image, individuals are empowered to pursue their aspirations boldly, knowing that they possess the resilience and determination to overcome obstacles along the way.

4. Better Physical Health Outcomes

The intricate link between mental and physical health underscores the importance of addressing psychological well-being in promoting overall wellness. Mental health counseling plays a vital role in this regard, as it helps individuals mitigate the detrimental effects of stress on the body. By learning stress management techniques and adopting healthier coping mechanisms, individuals can reduce their susceptibility to stress-related illnesses such as cardiovascular disease, hypertension, and gastrointestinal disorders. Furthermore, counseling promotes better sleep hygiene and encourages individuals to make healthier lifestyle choices, thereby enhancing their overall physical well-being.

5. Support During Life Transitions and Challenges

Life is replete with transitions and challenges, ranging from career changes and relocation to loss and trauma. During these turbulent times, mental health counseling offers a steady anchor, providing individuals with the support and guidance they need to navigate these complexities with resilience and grace. Therapists offer a compassionate ear and practical coping strategies, empowering individuals to confront adversity with strength and resilience. Whether mourning the loss of a loved one, adjusting to a new phase of life, or grappling with past trauma, individuals find solace and healing in the therapeutic process, emerging stronger and more resilient in the face of adversity.

6. Empowerment and Self-actualization

At its core, mental health counseling is a journey of self-discovery and empowerment. By delving into their values, beliefs, and passions, individuals gain a deeper understanding of themselves and the world around them. Through introspection and exploration, they unearth their authentic selves, aligning their actions with their innermost desires and aspirations. This process of self-actualization fosters a profound sense of empowerment, as individuals reclaim agency over their lives and chart a course towards fulfillment and authenticity. Armed with a newfound sense of purpose and clarity, individuals embark on a journey of personal growth and transformation, embracing the boundless possibilities that lie ahead.

Overcoming Barriers to Seeking Mental Health Counseling

Overcoming barriers to seeking mental health counseling is crucial for individuals to access the support they need. Here's how we can address these obstacles:

Destigmatizing Mental Health: By promoting open conversations and raising awareness about mental health issues, we can reduce stigma and encourage people to seek help without fear of judgment.

Improving Access to Services: Investing in affordable and accessible mental health care services, including teletherapy options and community resources, ensures that everyone can access the support they need, regardless of their financial situation or location.

Providing Education and Information: Offering information about the benefits of counseling, available resources, and how to navigate the mental health system empowers individuals to take proactive steps towards seeking help.

Supporting Cultural Competence: Recognizing and respecting cultural differences and providing culturally competent care ensures that mental health services are inclusive and accessible to diverse communities.

Addressing Systemic Barriers: Advocating for policy changes and systemic reforms to address underlying issues such as inequalities in healthcare access and social determinants of health ensures that everyone has equitable access to mental health support.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is your current wait time?

 

Our wait time is contingent on the therapist you choose. Some have current availability while others may be at capacity or unable to accept new clients at this time. When you call in we will work with you to make sure you are seen as soon as possible.

What types of therapies do you provide?

Types of evidence-based therapy we use either solely or in combination are the following:

  • ESFT—Eco-systemic structural family therapy—This type of therapy is useful for working with families and children that are challenged with behavioral and relationship issues.

  • TF-CBT –Trauma Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy—This is for individuals impacted by single, multiple, or complex trauma who need to process the events of the trauma safely and who also could benefit from coping skills to work through their experiences.

  • Person-Centered- This type of therapy is utilized to better understand your thoughts and feelings. The emphasis is placed on you as a person and as the expert in all things related to you.

  • Solution-Focused—This form of therapy focuses less on past life events but rather helps you to find ways in which you can use practical, useful strategies to deal with whatever concerns or challenges you have at the time.

  • Holistic health—This type of counseling takes into consideration your entire being and focuses on your mental, physical, and spiritual self in order to aid in the healing process. This is why we utilize treatments such as massage therapy and reiki to aid in physical and spiritual healing.

What payment types and insurances do you accept?

 

We accept Visa, MasterCard, Discover, American Express, cash, and checks.

We are currently paneled with most insurances such as: Horizon Blue Cross, Optum, Highmark, Cigna, United, and Premiere Blue Shield. We are currently not accepting Medicaid or Medicare at this time. Additionally, we are unable to accept cash or out of pocket payments if you have Medicaid or Medicare. If you do not see your insurance on the list please inquire online or via phone.

Are we accepting new clients?

 

Yes we are. You can book by clicking ‘Get Started’.

How long is a therapy session?

 

On average you can expect a session to last anywhere between 30 minutes to an hour. Sessions are scheduled according to your needs and goals. Some clients are seen weekly, some bi-weekly, while others are seen multiple times a week.

Additionally, clients usually can remain in treatment anywhere from 8 weeks to 1 year and sometimes longer. It all depends on your goals and your progress towards your goals. The key for us is to remain in constant communication with you with regards to your progress, establish a plan of how to attain those goals, and provide you with the tools to be able to achieve the vision.
