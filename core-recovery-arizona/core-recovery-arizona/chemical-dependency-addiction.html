Understanding Anxiety: Symptoms, Causes, and Types

Anxiety is more than just an occasional feeling of worry or fear. It’s a mental health condition that can disrupt daily life, making even simple tasks feel insurmountable. Understanding the symptoms, causes, and types of anxiety is the first step toward seeking the help you need.

Symptoms of Anxiety - People experiencing anxiety can display a variety of physical and emotional symptoms. Some common signs include disrupted appetite leading to weight fluctuations, irregular sleep patterns, fatigue, difficulty focusing, and indecisiveness. Some individuals may experience increased anxiety, while others might notice a decrease. Regardless, these fluctuations often put people’s health at risk.

Causes of Anxietyn - The exact cause of anxiety disorders isn’t fully understood, but it’s likely a combination of several factors. These may include genetics, brain chemistry, personality, and life events. For instance, traumatic experiences such as physical or emotional abuse, the death of a loved one, or a serious accident can trigger anxiety disorders in people who are already prone to anxiety.

Types of Anxiety Disorders - Anxiety disorders include several types, each with distinct symptoms and characteristics. They include generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), characterized by chronic anxiety and worries about everyday life events with no apparent reasons; panic disorder, marked by recurrent, unexpected panic attacks; social anxiety disorder, which involves overwhelming worry and self-consciousness about everyday social situations; and specific phobias, an intense fear of a specific object or situation.

Understanding anxiety and its symptoms is a critical step in seeking help. If you’re experiencing any of these symptoms or believe you may have an anxiety disorder, it might be time to reach out to a trusted therapist or counselor. At Core Recovery, we are ready to assist you in your journey towards a happier, healthier life.

A therapist, simply put, is your ally in your fight against anxiety. They are equipped with the knowledge, skills, and tools to help you manage and overcome anxiety.

A Therapeutic Relationship - The therapeutic relationship between you and your therapist is central to the process. This relationship is characterized by trust, empathy, and understanding. Your therapist creates a safe and nurturing environment where you can openly discuss your fears and worries. At Core Recovery, we understand that every individual is unique, hence we ensure that our therapists tailor their approach to suit your specific needs and goals.

Treatment Plans - A therapist for anxiety in Phoenix will work with you to develop a personalized treatment plan. This plan is usually a blend of different therapeutic approaches that have been proven effective in managing anxiety, such as Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and mindfulness-based cognitive therapy. The choice of therapy would depend on your unique needs and the severity of your anxiety.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long are Core Recovery's IOP and PHP programs?

We create an individual treatment plan for each patient, so the length of the program is tailored based on our patient’s clinical assessment and designed to create a transition to a happier and more rewarding life. On average our patients’ program at Core Recovery between three and six months.

Why should I choose one of Core Recovery’s IOP or PHP over another IOP or PHP?

Core Recovery is set apart from the other IOP and PHP programs in that we are an integrated behavioral health program that is individually customized for you. We use evidenced based modalities including EMDR, DBT, CBT and ACT depending on your specific needs. We offer group counseling (processing and psycho- education), therapeutic art, individual counseling, family counseling, and psychiatric medication management, all in-house. We also have an in-house lab to support sobriety and to monitor the medications that are prescribed to you.

Our multidisciplinary treatment team meets once a week where we collaborate as we identify ways to support you meeting your treatment goals. And not only do we take you into consideration, but we consider your loved ones who are supporting you in your journey to recovery in the family systems portion of our program. We understand that managing both treatment and your personal life can be stressful. That is why we have intentionally procured a caring and dedicated clinical staff with flexible schedules which include daytime and evening hours for both groups and individual therapy. This makes scheduling a breeze.

Can I see a counselor at Core without doing groups?

Groups are the foundation of our program and are required at all levels of care. The number of groups will be prescribed to you at your initial mental health intake appointment. Once you are a patient here at Core you may meet with one of our clinicians for a weekly 60-minute individual counseling, 60-minute family systems counseling and your 30-minute psychiatric/medication appointment.
