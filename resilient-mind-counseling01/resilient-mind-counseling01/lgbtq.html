Other Noteworthy BIPOC Therapists in Asheville, NC - While Resilient Mind Counseling provides a beacon of support for BIPOC individuals seeking therapy in Asheville, it’s important to highlight other valuable resources available. Let’s explore other practices that share a similar commitment to culturally aware mental health services.

Be BOLD Psychology and Consulting: Culturally Aware Therapy and Relationship Support - Be BOLD Psychology and Consulting is another noteworthy practice in North Carolina that offers culturally aware therapy and relationship support. This practice is led by a diverse team of professionals who are committed to providing culturally aware therapy and relationship support for BIPOC clients. Their services include group therapy, couples, relationship, and family therapy, as well as therapy for chronic pain, illness, and caregiver fatigue.

Be BOLD emphasizes the importance of therapy sessions that are driven by clients, encouraging open dialogue and accountability, recognizing that the client is the expert of their own life. The benefit of such an approach is that it allows BIPOC individuals to feel seen, heard, and understood in their experiences.

Other Directories for Finding BIPOC Clinicians in Asheville, NC - Aside from individual practices, there are also several online directories that can help BIPOC individuals find therapists who are aware and respectful of their unique experiences. One such platform is Inclusive Therapists, which provides a safer, simpler way to find care. This platform categorizes therapists by a range of services including individual therapy, family therapy, group therapy, and even medication management or prescription.

Inclusive Therapists also allows for filtering by specific community needs, making it easier for BIPOC, LGBTQ, neurodivergent, and disabled individuals to find affirming therapists who can provide the support they need. This platform is a valuable resource for those seeking culturally sensitive mental health services in Asheville, North Carolina.

The Cost of Therapy in North Carolina - Understanding the cost of therapy is crucial in making an informed decision about seeking mental health services. The cost of therapy can be a significant factor that can either enable or hinder access to essential mental health support.

General Counseling Rates in North Carolina - As per the prevailing rates in North Carolina, counseling services can vary based on the type of service, the length of the session, and the experience or specialty of the therapist involved. On average, session costs in North Carolina generally range from $120 to $175. This range covers a broad variety of therapy forms, from individual counseling sessions to specialized therapy services such as anxiety treatment, depression treatment, and mood disorder treatment, among others.

It’s important to note that while cost can be a barrier for some, many therapy providers accept insurance, which can significantly reduce out-of-pocket costs for clients. The inclusion of insurance providers such as Blue Cross/Blue Shield, United, Aetna, and Medcost among others can help make therapy more accessible for those in need.

Frequently Asked Questions

I’m scared that if I open up, you will think I’m crazy.

If you open up, we will not think you are crazy. We will think you are someone who’s very courageous. Many clients have had the same fear (that people would think they are crazy).

Through our own life journeys and working with clients, we know how “normal” your experience is.

You’re not being dramatic or “too much.” Nothing you can say will cause us to judge you.

We know nobody’s perfect. We all struggle. We all suffer in different ways.

Whatever it is you’re going through, we provide a safe space where you can feel heard and supported without judgment.

I’m nervous about starting therapy. Is that normal?

Therapy can be vulnerable and new, so it’s natural to be nervous. It can be scary to think of opening up or crying in front of someone new, perhaps fearing that it will be awkward and uncomfortable. The fear around that can feel overwhelming. Getting the counseling we need can be challenging. And although it’s normal to be nervous, that doesn’t make it feel great. It takes courage to continue getting the help you need.

Therapy is a space where you can be yourself and feel safe from judgment, including feeling nervous. We’re committed to finding a way to care for your nervousness and ensure sessions are as comfortable as possible.

We’re here if you want to talk about it during a free phone consultation.

How can I get started?

We’re here to talk during a free phone consultation where you can tell a therapist what’s up and decide together how they can help.

Call us today to get your free phone consultation at (828) 515-1246 or Contact Us.

What can I expect during the first session?

Overall, you can think of the first session as a structured assessment that helps your therapist get to know you and the challenges that brought you into treatment. They’ll invite you to share more about yourself, and you can also talk about what’s worked or not worked for your mental health in the past.

Some questions that your therapist might ask during a first session include:

  • What brought you to therapy?
  • Have you gone to therapy before? How did that go?
  • What do I need to know about you to understand your current challenges?

You can ask your therapist questions as well. You might be curious about their experience or how future sessions work.

The most important part of a first session is that you see if you feel your therapist is a good fit for you. If you feel supported, seen, and at ease with them, you can discuss if you’d like to meet again.

Does what we talk about in therapy remain confidential?

You might already know that you want to share sensitive information about your loved ones and you don’t want them to find out what you’ve said. So we want you to know that unless there is an imminent danger to yourself or someone else, everything you share is completely confidential.

There is nothing more important than your privacy.

You can rest assured that you can talk about anything without fear of that information getting out.

If you have specific questions about how confidentiality works, you can ask during your free phone consultation or anytime during your work with your therapist.
