Neurodivergent Therapy at Resilient Mind Counseling - Entering the safe space of Resilient Mind Counseling, you’ll find an array of services specifically tailored for neurodivergent individuals.

Overview of Services Offered - Resilient Mind Counseling is committed to providing comprehensive mental health services to individuals throughout North Carolina. We offer online therapy for teens and adults alike, addressing a variety of conditions including anxiety, depression, PTSD, mood disorders, and more. We are particularly focused on providing therapy for neurodivergent individuals, understanding the unique challenges that come with conditions such as ADHD, ADD, Autism, Complex PTSD, and others.

Beyond the usual therapy services, we also offer specialized services that cater to unique populations. These include LGBT therapy, addressing the unique challenges faced by the community, and BIPOC therapy, which takes into account the specific mental health struggles faced by Black, Indigenous, and People Of Color.

The Approach to Neurodivergent Therapy - Our approach in providing therapy for neurodivergent individuals is rooted in respect, understanding, and inclusivity. We understand that neurodivergent individuals often feel misunderstood or judged in traditional therapy settings. Hence, we strive to create an environment where they can feel seen, heard, and valued.

At Resilient Mind Counseling, we believe that neurodivergence is not a problem to be fixed, but a difference to be understood and accommodated. We approach therapy from the perspective of neurodiversity, acknowledging the different ways in which people’s brains function and learning to work with these differences rather than against them.

Addressing Common Issues in Neurodivergent Therapy - Neurodivergent individuals often face unique challenges. They may struggle with executive functioning, making it difficult to complete tasks, stay organized, or manage household chores. Sensory overload can also be an issue, with overwhelming sounds, sights, or social situations causing distress.

Our therapists work with clients to address these and other issues, helping them to develop coping mechanisms and strategies that work for them. We understand that neurodivergent individuals often think and process information differently, and we tailor our therapy to accommodate these differences.

At Resilient Mind Counseling, we also understand the importance of support and validation. We strive to provide a safe and welcoming space where neurodivergent individuals can feel accepted and understood, and where they can learn to celebrate their unique ways of thinking and processing the world.

In conclusion, at Resilient Mind Counseling, we believe in the power of neurodivergent-friendly therapy. We understand the unique challenges faced by neurodivergent individuals, and we are committed to providing the support and guidance they need to lead fulfilling lives.

The Benefits of Online Therapy for Neurodivergent Individuals - Accessibility and Convenience of Online Therapy:

For neurodivergent individuals, the traditional therapy model may pose certain challenges such as sensory overload, social anxiety, or difficulty with change and transitions. Online therapy, popularized in the wake of the global pandemic, offers an innovative solution to these issues, providing a more accessible and convenient approach to mental health care. With the advent of new technology, therapists can now meet clients in the comfort of their own homes, eliminating the need for commuting, dealing with weather conditions, or having to navigate unfamiliar environments.

Many clients find online therapy easier, more convenient, and more accessible than in-person sessions. The flexibility offered by virtual therapy allows individuals to fit sessions into their busy schedules without having to sacrifice other important aspects of their lives. Moreover, online therapy provides a level of privacy that is hard to match with in-person sessions. You have complete control over who knows about your treatment, and you can choose the time and location that suits you best, thereby creating a safe and comfortable space for your therapy sessions.

How Online Therapy Works at Resilient Mind Counseling - At Resilient Mind Counseling, we offer comprehensive online therapy services throughout Asheville and North Carolina for teens and adults alike. Our therapists are specialists in addressing neurodivergent struggles, among other mental health issues. We strive to accommodate our clients’ preferences and needs, offering a variety of therapy forms including neurodivergent therapy, depression treatment, anxiety treatment, and PTSD treatment.

Our online therapy process is straightforward and user-friendly. After reaching out to us, we schedule your first appointment with one of our online therapists. During these sessions, you receive the support and understanding you need in the privacy of your own space. We see neurodivergent individuals not as individuals with a problem to be fixed, but as individuals with unique brains that function in beautiful and extraordinary ways. We aim to support and guide you on your journey, acknowledging your struggles, but also celebrating your strengths and uniqueness.

Frequently Asked Questions

I’m scared that if I open up, you will think I’m crazy.

If you open up, we will not think you are crazy. We will think you are someone who’s very courageous. Many clients have had the same fear (that people would think they are crazy).

Through our own life journeys and working with clients, we know how “normal” your experience is.

You’re not being dramatic or “too much.” Nothing you can say will cause us to judge you.

We know nobody’s perfect. We all struggle. We all suffer in different ways.

Whatever it is you’re going through, we provide a safe space where you can feel heard and supported without judgment.

I’m nervous about starting therapy. Is that normal?

Therapy can be vulnerable and new, so it’s natural to be nervous. It can be scary to think of opening up or crying in front of someone new, perhaps fearing that it will be awkward and uncomfortable. The fear around that can feel overwhelming. Getting the counseling we need can be challenging. And although it’s normal to be nervous, that doesn’t make it feel great. It takes courage to continue getting the help you need.

Therapy is a space where you can be yourself and feel safe from judgment, including feeling nervous. We’re committed to finding a way to care for your nervousness and ensure sessions are as comfortable as possible.

We’re here if you want to talk about it during a free phone consultation.

How can I get started?

We’re here to talk during a free phone consultation where you can tell a therapist what’s up and decide together how they can help.

Call us today to get your free phone consultation at (828) 515-1246 or Contact Us.

What can I expect during the first session?

Overall, you can think of the first session as a structured assessment that helps your therapist get to know you and the challenges that brought you into treatment. They’ll invite you to share more about yourself, and you can also talk about what’s worked or not worked for your mental health in the past.

Some questions that your therapist might ask during a first session include:

  • What brought you to therapy?
  • Have you gone to therapy before? How did that go?
  • What do I need to know about you to understand your current challenges?

You can ask your therapist questions as well. You might be curious about their experience or how future sessions work.

The most important part of a first session is that you see if you feel your therapist is a good fit for you. If you feel supported, seen, and at ease with them, you can discuss if you’d like to meet again.

Does what we talk about in therapy remain confidential?

You might already know that you want to share sensitive information about your loved ones and you don’t want them to find out what you’ve said. So we want you to know that unless there is an imminent danger to yourself or someone else, everything you share is completely confidential.

There is nothing more important than your privacy.

You can rest assured that you can talk about anything without fear of that information getting out.

If you have specific questions about how confidentiality works, you can ask during your free phone consultation or anytime during your work with your therapist.
