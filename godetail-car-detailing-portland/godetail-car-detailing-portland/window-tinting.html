The Scratching Issue: Can Car Detailing Fix it?

So, to answer our key question: “Will car detailing remove scratches?” Yes, it can, but there’s a bit more to it. Not all scratches are created equal, and understanding the type of scratch is important.

Clear Coat Scratches: These are superficial scratches on the surface layer of your car’s paintwork. They’re the easiest to treat in the car detailing process, especially during the polishing stage.

Primer Scratches: These scratches penetrate deeper into your vehicle’s body, reaching the primer layer. They’re more challenging to address but can often be minimized through professional detailing.

Paint Scratches: The deepest scratches that reach your car’s paint layer are the hardest to deal with. Depending on their severity, car detailing may help lessen their appearance, but some may require more intensive repairs.

Detailers use various techniques to minimize the appearance of scratches, from buffing with a high-quality car polish to using a specialized scratch remover compound. For more severe damage, we provide paint protection film to further guard your vehicle’s body.
