Anxiety is a common condition that affects millions of people worldwide.

While some anxiety symptoms, like panic attacks, may be obvious, others can be more subtle. High-functioning anxiety is a type of anxiety that involves seemingly managing life well on the outside despite feeling anxious internally.

However, they may struggle with intense worry, racing thoughts, irritability, trouble sleeping, and other anxiety symptoms that impact their emotional well-being.

Here Are 7 Signs Of High-Functioning Anxiety You Shouldn’t Ignore:Excessive Worry One common trait of all types of anxiety, including high-functioning, is excessive and unrelenting worry.

You may worry about various aspects of life, such as your health, finances, career, family life, etc.

This worrying persists even when there’s no real reason to be concerned and feels uncontrollable and intrusive. This constant state of worry can be incredibly draining, making it challenging to get through each day.

A mental health professional can provide you with strategies to manage pervasive worrying and offer support as you work through your anxiety.

Perfectionism Perfectionism is a trait that can often overlap with high-functioning anxiety and cause significant distress.

Holding oneself and others to extremely high standards and becoming consumed by details are common signs of perfectionism.

However, when taken too far, perfectionism can impair one’s ability to complete tasks or try new things due to the fear of making mistakes. This, paired with high-functioning anxiety, often intensifies feelings of worry and being overwhelmed. It is essential to notice these patterns in oneself and seek help if problematic perfectionism is hindering daily life.

Difficulty Relaxing For those with anxiety, relaxation is often very difficult. The mind races even during downtime, constantly turning to thoughts of unfinished tasks, unsolved problems, worries, or fears.

Quieting both the mind and body may seem impossible with the constant intrusion of anxious thoughts. Activities like meditation, deep breathing exercises, and yoga that require turning focus inward and achieving calm can be incredibly challenging for the anxious mind.

Little by little, the grip of anxiety loosens, and the mind and body learn to relax and unwind. Though difficult, relaxation is not impossible for those with anxiety.

Irritability Or Agitation Anxiety can often lead to extreme irritability or agitation, making even minor irritations seem disproportionately frustrating and provoking anger quickly. Restless energy may be present just below the surface, manifesting internally and directed outwardly. Frequent muscle tension caused by constant agitation can exacerbate irritability, making you more prone to lashing out.

It may feel like running on fumes, and taking steps to manage this agitation can help stabilize your mood. Engaging in activities such as exercise, deep breathing exercises, meditation, or counseling sessions with a mental health professional can help alleviate these symptoms.

Feeling Overwhelmed Easily One of the symptoms of high-functioning anxiety is feeling overwhelmed quickly. Even small commitments can add up and induce feelings of being overwhelmed.

It becomes increasingly difficult to handle what used to be a normal workload when dealing with anxiety. If you find your stamina for handling life’s challenges is much lower than usual, it may be time to pay attention and take action.

Fees and Insurance

At Channeling Growth Therapy, we firmly believe in accessible mental health support, and this commitment is reflected in our flexible payment and insurance policy. We understand that financial considerations often play a crucial role in your decision to seek therapeutic services. That’s why we strive to ensure our services are not just of the highest quality but also financially accessible to you.

We are delighted to inform you that we are in-network with a variety of health insurance providers. This includes Beacon/Carelon Health Insurance, MHN Medi-Cal, and Cigna insurance. This means that if you are insured by any of these providers, your insurance plan may cover part or even the entire cost of the therapeutic services that we offer.

We encourage you to reach out to your insurance provider to verify your coverage for mental health services. They will provide detailed information on your deductible, copayments, and any out-of-pocket expenses that may be applicable.

In case you don’t have insurance or your plan doesn’t cover our services, please reach out to us. We are committed to working with you to ensure you receive the help you need. We can discuss our fees and explore payment plans to find an arrangement that works best for you.
