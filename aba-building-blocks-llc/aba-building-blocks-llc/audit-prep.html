Becoming an RBT is not just about obtaining a certification—it’s about equipping yourself to make a significant difference in the lives of individuals with developmental behavior challenges. It’s a rewarding career path that allows you to use ABA principles to bring about meaningful change in your clients’ lives.

As you embark on your journey to become an RBT, remember that the team at ABA Building Blocks is here to support you every step of the way. We are dedicated to ensuring you receive the quality training you need to excel in this vital role.

Requirements for RBT Certification - Before you start your RBT certification online, there are a few prerequisites you need to meet. These requirements ensure that you have the necessary foundation to understand and implement the principles of Applied Behavior Analysis effectively.

Age and Education Requirements - Firstly, to be eligible for RBT certification, you must be at least 18 years old and have a high school diploma or equivalent. These requirements ensure that you have the basic educational foundation necessary for understanding the coursework involved in RBT training.

Background Check - Next, you must successfully pass a background check. As an RBT, you will be working closely with sensitive populations, including children and individuals with various behavioral conditions. The background check is a crucial step to ensure the safety and well-being of these individuals.

40-Hour Training Course - Once you meet these prerequisites, you can start your RBT training, which includes a 40-hour training course. This course is comprehensive and covers all the tasks in the 2nd Edition Registered Behavior Technician ™ Task List.

Understanding the 2nd Edition Task List - The 2nd Edition Task List outlines the core responsibilities and skills you need to master as an RBT. It includes areas like measurement, assessment, skill acquisition, behavior reduction, documentation and reporting, and professional conduct and scope of practice. Our RBT training at ABA Building Blocks provides clear instruction, video models, and interactive activities to ensure you understand and can apply these tasks effectively.

Time Frame for Completing the Training - The training course must be completed within 180 days. It ensures that you receive a concentrated and thorough understanding of the principles and practices of Applied Behavior Analysis. The goal isn’t just to complete the course—it’s to gain the knowledge and skills you need to make a positive impact in your clients’ lives.

In the next section, we’ll take a closer look at how you can get your RBT certification online. But before we do, remember that our team at ABA Building Blocks is here to support you on this journey. We’re committed to helping you succeed and make a difference in the lives of those you serve.

How to Get RBT Certification Online - Embarking on your journey to obtain RBT certification online can seem daunting, but don’t worry, we’re here to guide you. This process can be divided into two primary steps: choosing the right online RBT training program and successfully navigating the online training process.

Choosing the Right Online RBT Training Program - When it comes to online RBT training, you have several options.

Accredited Online RBT Programs - Accredited programs are a great place to start. They have the approval of relevant accrediting bodies, ensuring that the course content aligns with the required training standards. At ABA Building Blocks, we offer a comprehensive 40-hour course that covers the 2nd Edition Registered Behavior Technician ™ Task List. The course includes clear instruction, video models, and interactive application activities making it comprehensive, interesting, and practical.

Free Online RBT Training Options - There are also free online RBT training options available. While these might not provide the same level of comprehensive training as accredited programs, they can be a good place to start if you’re on a tight budget. Just remember, it’s crucial to ensure that the course you choose covers all the necessary content listed on the RBT Task List.

Navigating the Online Training Process - Once you’ve chosen your RBT training program, it’s time to start studying!

Interactive Learning Activities - Engaging with the course content is crucial. On platforms like BehaviorTechCourse, you’ll find a range of activities aimed at deepening your understanding, such as reading articles, participating in discussion boards, and completing assignments.

Assessment and Quizzes - At the end of each section, you’ll usually find quizzes. These are designed to assess your grasp of the content. You’ll need to score at least 80% to proceed, but most platforms allow multiple attempts.

Final Test and Certification - After completing all the training modules, you will take a final test combining all the quizzes. You can retake this final test as many times as necessary until you pass. Once you’ve passed, you can request your course completion certificate, marking the end of your online RBT training.
