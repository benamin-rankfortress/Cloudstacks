Registered Behavior Technicians work directly with clients, implementing the behavior intervention plans created by Board Certified Behavior Analysts (BCBAs). RBTs spend more time with clients than any other team member, working in homes, schools, and clinics. They work on reducing problematic behaviors and teaching new skills to clients, using evidence-based ABA principles.

The demand for RBTs has exploded. The RBT credential is recognized in all 50 states and Canada, and it’s often a requirement set by insurance companies for the treatment of children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

The Scope of Work for an RBT - RBTs have a broad scope of work. They are involved in direct implementation of behavior-analytic services, which include:

Measurement: RBTs collect data and conduct certain types of assessments (e.g., stimulus preference assessments).

Assessment: They assist with individualized assessment procedures (e.g., functional assessment) and conduct or assist with stimulus preference assessments.

Skill Acquisition: RBTs carry out skill acquisition procedures, teaching new behaviors and skills to clients.

Behavior Reduction: They implement behavior reduction plans, working to decrease problematic behaviors.

Documentation and Reporting: RBTs need to document and report their sessions accurately, adhering to data collection and session note requirements.

Ethics and Professional Conduct: RBTs are expected to maintain high ethical standards, complying with the RBT Ethics Code (2.0).

RBTs, however, do not design intervention or assessment plans. It’s the responsibility of the BCBA or other higher-level service provider.

Becoming an RBT is not just about obtaining a certification—it’s about equipping yourself to make a significant difference in the lives of individuals with developmental behavior challenges. It’s a rewarding career path that allows you to use ABA principles to bring about meaningful change in your clients’ lives.

As you embark on your journey to become an RBT, remember that the team at ABA Building Blocks is here to support you every step of the way. We are dedicated to ensuring you receive the quality training you need to excel in this vital role.

Requirements for RBT Certification - Before you start your RBT certification online, there are a few prerequisites you need to meet. These requirements ensure that you have the necessary foundation to understand and implement the principles of Applied Behavior Analysis effectively.

Age and Education Requirements - Firstly, to be eligible for RBT certification, you must be at least 18 years old and have a high school diploma or equivalent. These requirements ensure that you have the basic educational foundation necessary for understanding the coursework involved in RBT training.

Background Check - Next, you must successfully pass a background check. As an RBT, you will be working closely with sensitive populations, including children and individuals with various behavioral conditions. The background check is a crucial step to ensure the safety and well-being of these individuals.
