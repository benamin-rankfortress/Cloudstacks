Maintaining Your RBT Certification - Congratulations! You have completed your online RBT certification and are now a certified Registered Behavior Technician. But what’s next? How do you maintain your RBT certification? We at ABA Building Blocks are here to guide you through this process.

The RBT Ethics Code (2.0) - The first thing to keep in mind is the RBT Ethics Code (2.0). This set of standards outlines the ethical requirements that RBT certificants must adhere to in their professional practice. Violations of the RBT Ethics Code could result in the revocation of your certification. Thus, it’s crucial to familiarize yourself with these ethical standards and integrate them into your daily practice.

Continuing Education and Recertification - Keeping your knowledge and skills up-to-date is crucial for maintaining your RBT certification. This is where continuing education comes into play. Our expert, Kim Finger, Ph.D. recommends participating in ongoing professional development activities and learning opportunities.

The Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB) requires RBTs to recertify annually. This involves completing a renewal application, passing a competency assessment, and confirming adherence to the RBT Ethics Code.

As an RBT, your ability to deliver effective behavior-analytic services depends on your commitment to ongoing learning and ethical practice. The field of Applied Behavior Analysis is dynamic, and staying current with the latest research and best practices is key to your continued success.
