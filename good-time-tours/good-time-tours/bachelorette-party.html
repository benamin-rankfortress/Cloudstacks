Bachelorette Party

Stability in Design: A vessel with a stable hull minimizes rocking, which can be particularly comforting for those prone to motion sickness or who are less accustomed to maritime travel.

Shallow Draft for Easy Shore Access: For parties that involve swimming or beachside activities, choose a boat that can easily approach the shore.

Swim Platforms and Ladders: Facilitate safe and effortless water access with well-designed swim platforms and ladders, turning a simple dip into a stress-free activity.

Entertainment Amenities: A sound system for music and perhaps video equipment for movies can be invaluable during moments of downtime, keeping guests of all ages entertained.

Protective Shade Structures: Ensure there are ample shaded areas to shield guests from the sun’s intensity, a consideration that’s particularly important for children and seniors.

Varied Seating Options: Look for boats with a range of seating arrangements that offer both comfort and opportunities for social interaction.

Accessible Restroom Facilities: Essential for families, especially those with young children or expectant mothers, are on-board restrooms.

Equipped Galley: A kitchen area with refrigeration, a microwave, and proper food storage is indispensable for serving refreshments and meals throughout the event.

Incorporating these features will not only enhance the comfort and convenience of your boat party but will also ensure that it caters to the needs of family members across generations.

Safety Protocols and Equipment - The cornerstone of any family-friendly boat party is the assurance of safety for all on board. To this end, a comprehensive approach to safety protocols and equipment is non-negotiable.

Essential safety gear includes: Personal Flotation Devices (PFDs): Ensure there is a life jacket for every passenger, approved by the United States Coast Guard (USCG), and appropriate for each individual’s size and weight, including children.

Fire Extinguishers: Keep on hand fire extinguishers that are in good working order and suitable for potential onboard fire types.

First Aid Kit: A comprehensive first-aid kit is indispensable for addressing any medical needs that may arise.

VHF Radio and Emergency Communication Devices: These are crucial for emergency communication, as cell phone service can be unreliable on the water.

Visibility and Signaling Equipment: Navigation lights and sound-producing devices are essential for safe operation, particularly in low-visibility conditions.

Rescue Throwables: USCG-approved throwable flotation devices are vital for quick response in a man-overboard scenario.

Beyond equipment, safety procedures are equally important: Man Overboard Drills: Guests should be familiar with the steps to take in the event someone falls into the water.

Emergency Evacuation Protocols: A well-rehearsed evacuation plan is essential for quick and orderly disembarkation if needed.

Pre-Departure Safety Checks: Verify that all safety equipment is functional and accessible before embarking.

Clear Communication: Establish and maintain open lines of communication between guests and crew for effective direction during emergencies.

Local Waterway Knowledge: Familiarity with the local marine environment can help avoid accidents related to underwater hazards or unexpected weather changes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I make a reservation?

Make sure to give yourself plenty of time (at least 4-6 months in advance).  Reservations fill up quickly.

Where is the Pickup and Drop Off Location?

We will pick you up at Paradise Cove Marina located at 17141 Rocky Ridge Rd, Austin, Tx 78734, which is about 35 minutes from downtown Austin. After you enter the main entrance park wherever you can and then walk to the floating docks. The boats are located at boat slip #D9 and #D11

What Should We Bring on the Bachelorette Boat?

Guests can bring snacks, food and drinks. It’s BYOB. If drinking beer, wine or other alcohol make sure everyone is over 21. Other things to bring are towels, sunglasses, music playlist, sunblock.

Can I bring my own food and beverage?

Yes of course your choice of type of food. If drinking beer or other alcohol make sure everyone is over 21. We’re not afraid to card you.
