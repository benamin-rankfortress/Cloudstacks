Different Approaches to Family Therapy - Family therapy can be approached in several ways, and the approach taken often depends on the specific needs and dynamics of the family in focus. Here at Stickney Counseling, we are experienced in various evidence-based treatment approaches, and we can tailor our methods to suit your unique situation. Let’s take a closer look at four common approaches to family therapy.

Systemic Family Therapy - Systemic family therapy is one of the therapeutic approaches we use. This approach views the family as a unit, where each member’s actions affect the entire family. This therapy is aimed at understanding family processes, how they influence individuals, and how they change over time. The ultimate goal is to enhance family interactions and dynamics, thus improving everyone’s well-being.

Structural Family Therapy - Structural family therapy is another approach we use, developed in the 1960s by Salvador Minuchin. It’s based on the idea that children’s emotional and behavioral concerns are often linked to dysfunctional family structures. The focus of this therapy is to understand boundaries and subsystems within a family, allowing everyone to interact in more productive ways. It also emphasizes on developing appropriate boundaries and strengthening relationships among family members.

Brief Strategic Family Therapy - Brief strategic family therapy is a short-term approach, often limited to around 12 sessions. The goal is to identify and restructure the family interactions that lead to problematic behaviors, especially in a child, teen, or young adult. This method concentrates on strengthening positive patterns and modifying family behaviors that aren’t helpful, especially for a young person in crisis.

Psychoeducation - Psychoeducation is an integral part of family therapy. It involves learning more about mental health conditions that are affecting family relationships, along with evidence-based treatments for these conditions. Understanding these conditions can provide new insights, foster empathy, and improve the overall family dynamic.

These therapeutic approaches are just a few examples of the methods we use to help families navigate through challenges and improve their relationships. We are dedicated to providing family therapy that is tailored to suit your specific needs and circumstances.

Next, we will discuss how to choose a family therapist and what to consider when making this important decision. We believe that the right counselor plays a crucial role in maximizing the family therapy benefits you can experience.

How to Choose a Family Therapist - When choosing a family therapist, there are several key factors to consider. Making the right choice is vital to experiencing the full range of family therapy benefits.

Importance of Specialized Training in Family Therapy - A therapist’s qualifications and experience in family therapy are critical. This type of therapy requires a unique understanding of family dynamics and the skills to navigate complex relationships. The American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy (AAMFT) outlines that family therapists provide services such as diagnosis and treatment of mental health conditions, treatment planning, and psychotherapy for individuals, couples, and children. Therefore, ensure the therapist you choose has the necessary training and is registered with a professional body.

Finding a Therapist Who is a Good Fit for Your Family - Every family is unique, and find a therapist who resonates with your family’s needs and dynamics. This could involve several steps, such as discussing with potential therapists, checking their experience, and even scheduling a few initial sessions to see if they are a good fit. Trust your instincts and prioritize a therapist who makes your family feel comfortable and understood.

At Stickney Counseling, we understand the importance of finding the right fit. We encourage potential clients to schedule an initial consultation or phone call with us to discuss their concerns and our treatment approach.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can therapy help me?

 

Therapy can provide a range of benefits, such as:

  • Gaining insight and understanding into your thoughts, emotions, and behaviors.
  • Developing coping skills to manage stress, anxiety, or depression.
  • Improving communication and relationship skills.
  • Resolving conflicts or traumas from the past.
  • Enhancing self-esteem and personal growth.
  • Setting and achieving goals for personal or professional development.
Does insurance cover therapy?

 

Some of our therapists accept insurance. Many insurance plans cover mental health therapy, but coverage can vary. It's important to contact your insurance provider to understand your specific benefits, including coverage limits, copayments, and any required pre-authorization. You may also be able to submit session costs for out of network coverage.

How long does therapy typically last?

 

The duration of therapy varies depending on the individual and their specific needs. Some people may require only a few sessions to address a specific issue, while others may benefit from longer-term therapy to address deeper concerns or ongoing support. Therapists typically work collaboratively with clients to determine the appropriate duration of treatment.

How do I know if a therapist is a good fit for me?

 

Finding the right therapist is crucial for a successful therapeutic experience. It's recommended to schedule an initial consultation or phone call with potential therapists to discuss your concerns, treatment approach, and assess whether you feel comfortable and understood. Trust your instincts and choose a therapist with whom you feel a good rapport and connection.
