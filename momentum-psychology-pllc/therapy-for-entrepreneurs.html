Setting Priorities Based on These Values - Once youve identified your core values, you can start setting priorities based on them. This doesnt mean that work should always take a backseat to family, or vice versa. Instead, its about finding a balance that aligns with your values and enhances your overall quality of life.

For example, if one of your core values is family, you might prioritize having breakfast with your family or scheduling phone-free hours after work to focus on your kids. If career growth is a high priority, you might choose to take advantage of quiet morning hours at the office to accomplish key tasks.

Your priorities arent set in stone. They can and should shift as your circumstances, goals, and values evolve. Open communication with your family and employer can help manage expectations and ensure that youre all on the same page.

At Momentum Psychology, we understand that managing work and family demands can be challenging. But by identifying your core values and setting priorities based on them, you can create a balance that feels right for you.

The Benefits of Planning Ahead - Having a plan for your week or even your day can help you stay organized and reduce the feeling of being overwhelmed. It allows you to distribute your time and energy better, ensuring that both your work and family responsibilities are addressed.

By planning meals, scheduling tasks, and even allocating time for relaxation or personal care, you can take control of your time and reduce the pressure that comes with rushed decisions or last-minute challenges.

Strategies for Effective Planning - Effective planning requires a bit of time investment, but the pay-off is well worth it. Here are a few strategies that can help:

1. Keep a Family Calendar: Having a family calendar that is accessible to all family members can keep everyone in the loop about upcoming appointments, events, or deadlines. This can help distribute responsibilities and ensure that nothing falls through the cracks.

2. Meal Prep: Meal preparation can be a significant time saver during the workweek. Consider setting aside time on the weekend to prepare meals for the week and store them in the refrigerator or freezer. This can also create an opportunity for family bonding time if older children are involved in the cooking process.

3. To-Do Lists: Create a list of tasks that need to be done each day or week. Prioritize them based on their urgency and importance. This can help ensure that you are focusing your energy on the things that matter most.

4. Set Time for Relaxation: It may seem counterintuitive, but scheduling time for relaxation can actually help improve your productivity and stress levels. This might mean setting aside time for exercise, personal hobbies, or simply unwinding with a good book.

5. Plan for the Unexpected: Life is unpredictable, so have a plan B (or even C) for when things dont go as planned. Having backup childcare options, flexible work arrangements, or extra meals prepared can help manage unexpected situations without causing too much disruption.

The goal of planning is not to create a rigid structure, but to provide a framework that can help you manage your time and responsibilities more effectively. By planning and preparing in advance, youre not just organizing your time youre actively taking steps on how to manage work and family demands.

Tip 3: Negotiate for Flexible Work Arrangements - Balancing work and family can often feel like walking a tightrope. Its not easy, but with the right strategies, you can find a balance that works for you. One such strategy is negotiating for flexible work arrangements.

The Advantages of Flexible Work Hours - Flexible work hours can offer a multitude of benefits both for you and your employer. For one, it allows you to work during your most productive hours, leading to increased efficiency and productivity. Moreover, it enables you to attend to family commitments, such as school drop-offs and pick-ups, without sacrificing your work time.

Flexible work arrangements also reduce stress and improve work-life balance, leading to higher job satisfaction. It can even save commuting time, which can be repurposed for family activities or self-care. Some companies even offer four 10-hour workdays instead of five, providing employees with an extra day off to spend with their family.

How to Approach Your Employer About Flexible Work Arrangements - If you think flexible work arrangements could help you manage work and family demands, the first step is to talk to your employer. Start by doing your research. Understand what flexible work arrangements are feasible for your role and the impact it could have on your team or company.

Once you have a clear idea, schedule a meeting with your boss. In this meeting, clearly express your needs and propose a flexible work schedule that suits both your personal needs and the companys requirements. Highlight the benefits this could bring to the organization, such as improved productivity and morale.

How do I schedule an appointment?

Current Clients

If you are a current client, you can just log into the Client Portal to schedule an appointment unless your provider is full and running a waitlist. Here’s a helpful guide on how to request appointments.

If you see no appointments listed, then that means that there are no times available for your therapist. Please discuss with your therapist or contact the Client Services Coordinator as necessary to figure out how to schedule.

New Clients

If you are interested in becoming a new client, please complete our Appointment Request Form.

Please fill out the request form completely to ensure that we can best serve your needs. Please include a brief description of your problem, the name of the therapist that you would like to work with, and other details required by the form.

Once you have completed the form, our providers will review your information to see if:

  • You’re a good fit for our practice

  • If you indicated a preferred provider, whether that provider is accepting new clients, and if not, who would be the best fit for you.

Please check the Services tab for a description of who we treat and the Focus tab for our primary practice areas. If you are struggling with a severe mental health issue or a severe major depressive episode, our practice is likely not the best fit for your needs. Please let us know, and we will provide a list of referrals.

If Your Chosen Provider is Running a Waitlist

If your preferred provider is currently running a waitlist, then you will be offered the following options:

1) Work with another provider in our office

2) Be placed on the provider’s waitlist

Our Client Coordinator doesn’t update people on their status on the list regularly. You will be contacted only when you’re one of the next 2 – 3 names on the list. Please contact our Client Coordinator if you have more questions.

If a provider is available, then our Client Care Coordinator will contact you with next steps for scheduling your intake.

Please note that you must complete paperwork 72 hours prior to your appointment due to new federal requirements under the No Surprises Act.

Can I choose my therapist?

If you have a specific therapist(s) you want to work with, you may request them when filling out the Appointment Request Form. For a list of our providers and their specialties, please visit our Team Page.

Our providers will review the information to determine who they feel may be the best fit for you. If we do not feel that one of our providers will be a good fit for you, we will do our best to provide you with a suggestion of a provider who will better fit your needs.

For telehealth clients who don’t live in our home states and don’t live in a PSYPACT state, we cannot provide you services.

However, we can provide our referral list that includes links to national specialist directories and we are also happy to check with our national referral networks.
