Understanding Online Therapy: A Convenient Solution for Busy Professionals

As high-achieving professionals, it’s often a challenge to find time for self-care amidst the daily hustle. One solution that has emerged over the years is online therapy, a convenient and flexible way to access mental health support from anywhere.

What is Online Therapy? - Online therapy, also known as teletherapy, is the process of meeting with a licensed mental health professional via electronic devices such as a computer, smartphone, or tablet. It allows for real-time, face-to-face interactions just as if you were sitting in the therapist’s office. This means you can attend therapy sessions from the comfort of your home, office, or while traveling, making it an ideal solution for busy executives who may have difficulty scheduling traditional therapy sessions.

The Benefits of Online Therapy for Executives - Online therapy offers several benefits that make it a viable option for executives. Firstly, it provides increased convenience and flexibility. Instead of trying to squeeze in a therapy session during office hours or battling traffic to reach a therapist’s office, you can simply log in from your desired location at a time that suits you best.

How Online Therapy Works: Platforms and Processes - Online therapy at Momentum Psychology utilizes secure online video conferencing to provide sessions. We use a HIPAA/HITECH compliant web-based system, IntakeQ, that is easy to access and user-friendly.

The process is simple. Once you’ve scheduled a session, you’ll receive a link to join the video call. At the scheduled time, you simply click the link to start the session, ensuring you’re in a private place where you won’t be disturbed. This secure platform ensures your privacy is maintained, and the sessions are as effective as in-person therapy.

Just like in traditional therapy, online sessions involve open discussions about your feelings, challenges, and goals. The Role of Online Therapy in Addressing Executive Mental Health High-achieving professionals often juggle a flurry of work and personal demands, an aspect that can take a toll on their mental health. With the increasing acceptance of therapy among executives – 32 percent consult with an executive counselor and 22 percent see a psychotherapist – it’s clear that mental health support is becoming more crucial for this demographic. Online therapy, with its convenience and accessibility, is particularly beneficial for executives who are constantly on the go.

The Prevalence of Mental Health Issues Among Executives - Despite the image of resilience and strength that many executives portray, mental health issues are not uncommon in this group. The pressures of leadership, decision-making, and work-life balance can lead to stress, anxiety, and even burnout. These challenges, if not addressed, can hinder an executive’s performance and overall well-being.

How Online Therapy Can Help Executives - Online therapy for executives in North Carolina can provide the necessary support to navigate these challenges. By connecting with a therapist via secure video conferencing, executives can avail themselves of therapy sessions at a time and place that suits their schedule. This adaptability removes the barriers of commuting and scheduling, making therapy a more feasible option.

Moreover, online therapy, according to the American Psychological Association, can be one of the most effective solutions for many problems affecting work and life. For issues like anxiety disorders and obsessive-compulsive disorder, therapy is often the first-line option, not medication.

At Momentum Psychology, we specialize in various therapeutic approaches suitable for executives, including Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), and Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT). These evidence-based treatments can help manage stress, address anxiety-related disorders, and navigate life transitions, all of which are common issues faced by executives.

How do I schedule an appointment?

Current Clients

If you are a current client, you can just log into the Client Portal to schedule an appointment unless your provider is full and running a waitlist. Here’s a helpful guide on how to request appointments.

If you see no appointments listed, then that means that there are no times available for your therapist. Please discuss with your therapist or contact the Client Services Coordinator as necessary to figure out how to schedule.

New Clients

If you are interested in becoming a new client, please complete our Appointment Request Form.

Please fill out the request form completely to ensure that we can best serve your needs. Please include a brief description of your problem, the name of the therapist that you would like to work with, and other details required by the form.

Once you have completed the form, our providers will review your information to see if:

  • You’re a good fit for our practice

  • If you indicated a preferred provider, whether that provider is accepting new clients, and if not, who would be the best fit for you.

Please check the Services tab for a description of who we treat and the Focus tab for our primary practice areas. If you are struggling with a severe mental health issue or a severe major depressive episode, our practice is likely not the best fit for your needs. Please let us know, and we will provide a list of referrals.

If Your Chosen Provider is Running a Waitlist

If your preferred provider is currently running a waitlist, then you will be offered the following options:

1) Work with another provider in our office

2) Be placed on the provider’s waitlist

Our Client Coordinator doesn’t update people on their status on the list regularly. You will be contacted only when you’re one of the next 2 – 3 names on the list. Please contact our Client Coordinator if you have more questions.

If a provider is available, then our Client Care Coordinator will contact you with next steps for scheduling your intake.

Please note that you must complete paperwork 72 hours prior to your appointment due to new federal requirements under the No Surprises Act.

Can I choose my therapist?

If you have a specific therapist(s) you want to work with, you may request them when filling out the Appointment Request Form. For a list of our providers and their specialties, please visit our Team Page.

Our providers will review the information to determine who they feel may be the best fit for you. If we do not feel that one of our providers will be a good fit for you, we will do our best to provide you with a suggestion of a provider who will better fit your needs.

For telehealth clients who don’t live in our home states and don’t live in a PSYPACT state, we cannot provide you services.

However, we can provide our referral list that includes links to national specialist directories and we are also happy to check with our national referral networks.
