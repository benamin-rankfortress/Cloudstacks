10 TIPS FOR BALANCING WORK AND FAMILY DEMANDS: Work and life feeling out of balance? It’s a struggle many of us face, and that’s especially true for high-achieving professionals. When the pressure’s on at work and responsibilities at home are mounting, everything seems to become a blur. How do you manage work and family demands without burning out or feeling overwhelmed?

Understanding the Importance of Work-Life Balance - At Momentum Psychology, we understand firsthand the demands of today’s fast-paced world. Balancing work and family life isn’t just about surviving—it’s about thriving. A harmonious balance leads to a happier, healthier lifestyle, strengthening relationships while promoting wellness and productivity. But navigating that balance can be tricky.

The Challenges of Balancing Work and Family Demands - Striking a good balance between your career and personal life requires clear boundaries, efficient time management, and thoughtful self-care. Technological advances and current work culture often blur this boundary, resulting in employees grappling with the constant connection to work.

Finding the optimal work-life balance is especially challenging for high-achieving professionals who are used to going above and beyond in all aspects of their life—lawyers, physicians, executives, entrepreneurs, and students.

Let’s take a snap look at the keys to moving towards a healthier work-life balance: Setting priorities: Determine what is vital and what can wait.

Creating boundaries: Keep work and personal life separate. - Investing in self-care: It’s crucial to keep yourself healthy and nurtured.

Building a support system: Leaning on loved ones or professional help can make a huge difference. - Maintaining flexibility: To accommodate the unpredictable nature of life.

Keep in mind fulfilling these steps is a dynamic process, and needs constant fine-tuning as circumstances change. Further in this article, we delve into each of these aspects in detail. But before we do, let’s take a glimpse into the challenges faced through an infographic.

In the end, remember, balancing work and family life isn’t about achieving perfection—it’s about making choices and adjustments that fuel joy, productivity, and meaningful relationships. It’s a journey we’re excited to embark on with you. Let’s dive into those in-depth tips.

Tip 1: Prioritize Based on Your Values - Identifying Your Core Values - One of the first steps in learning how to manage work and family demands is to identify your core values. These are the principles that guide your decisions and behaviors. They might include things like health, family, career growth, or personal development. Your core values are unique to you and reflect what you find most important in life.

Take some time to reflect on what truly matters to you. Ask yourself questions like: What do I want to be remembered for? What kind of parent, partner, or professional do I want to be? What are the non-negotiables in my life?

Setting Priorities Based on These Values - Once you’ve identified your core values, you can start setting priorities based on them. This doesn’t mean that work should always take a backseat to family, or vice versa. Instead, it’s about finding a balance that aligns with your values and enhances your overall quality of life.

For example, if one of your core values is family, you might prioritize having breakfast with your family or scheduling phone-free hours after work to focus on your kids. If career growth is a high priority, you might choose to take advantage of quiet morning hours at the office to accomplish key tasks.

Your priorities aren’t set in stone. They can and should shift as your circumstances, goals, and values evolve. Open communication with your family and employer can help manage expectations and ensure that you’re all on the same page.

How do I schedule an appointment?

Current Clients

If you are a current client, you can just log into the Client Portal to schedule an appointment unless your provider is full and running a waitlist. Here’s a helpful guide on how to request appointments.

If you see no appointments listed, then that means that there are no times available for your therapist. Please discuss with your therapist or contact the Client Services Coordinator as necessary to figure out how to schedule.

New Clients

If you are interested in becoming a new client, please complete our Appointment Request Form.

Please fill out the request form completely to ensure that we can best serve your needs. Please include a brief description of your problem, the name of the therapist that you would like to work with, and other details required by the form.

Once you have completed the form, our providers will review your information to see if:

  • You’re a good fit for our practice

  • If you indicated a preferred provider, whether that provider is accepting new clients, and if not, who would be the best fit for you.

Please check the Services tab for a description of who we treat and the Focus tab for our primary practice areas. If you are struggling with a severe mental health issue or a severe major depressive episode, our practice is likely not the best fit for your needs. Please let us know, and we will provide a list of referrals.

If Your Chosen Provider is Running a Waitlist

If your preferred provider is currently running a waitlist, then you will be offered the following options:

1) Work with another provider in our office

2) Be placed on the provider’s waitlist

Our Client Coordinator doesn’t update people on their status on the list regularly. You will be contacted only when you’re one of the next 2 – 3 names on the list. Please contact our Client Coordinator if you have more questions.

If a provider is available, then our Client Care Coordinator will contact you with next steps for scheduling your intake.

Please note that you must complete paperwork 72 hours prior to your appointment due to new federal requirements under the No Surprises Act.

Can I choose my therapist?

If you have a specific therapist(s) you want to work with, you may request them when filling out the Appointment Request Form. For a list of our providers and their specialties, please visit our Team Page.

Our providers will review the information to determine who they feel may be the best fit for you. If we do not feel that one of our providers will be a good fit for you, we will do our best to provide you with a suggestion of a provider who will better fit your needs.

For telehealth clients who don’t live in our home states and don’t live in a PSYPACT state, we cannot provide you services.

However, we can provide our referral list that includes links to national specialist directories and we are also happy to check with our national referral networks.
