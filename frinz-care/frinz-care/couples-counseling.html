Lifestyle Changes to Help Cope with PTSD - While professional help is crucial when dealing with PTSD, adopting certain lifestyle habits can also play a vital role in managing PTSD symptoms effectively. Recovery is not just about managing your symptoms—it’s also about nurturing your overall wellbeing.

The Importance of Physical Health in Managing PTSD - Physical health has a direct impact on your mental health. Simply put, when your body feels good, your mind does too. It’s important to prioritize your physical health as part of your PTSD management strategy.

Healthy Eating:

Start by adopting a healthy diet. Nutritious meals not only keep your energy up but also keep your mind clear. Foods rich in Omega-3s, such as fatty fish and flaxseed, play a vital role in emotional health. Limiting processed food, fried food, refined starches, and sugars can help manage mood swings and prevent energy fluctuations.

Adequate Sleep:

Sleep deprivation can exacerbate PTSD symptoms like anger and irritability. Aim for 7 to 9 hours of sleep each night. A relaxing bedtime ritual and a quiet, dark, soothing bedroom environment can help improve sleep quality.

Exercise:

Regular physical activity is also crucial. Exercise releases endorphins, natural mood lifters that can help combat PTSD symptoms. Whether it’s a brisk walk, yoga, or a more intense workout, find a routine that suits you and stick with it.

Engaging in Positive Activities as a Coping Mechanism - Staying active and engaged can also be beneficial when dealing with PTSD. Behavioral activation is a technique that involves increasing your engagement in positive and rewarding activities. This could include spending time with friends, exercising, volunteering, or even rearranging furniture. Set a goal and track your progress as you engage in these activities.

The Role of Social Support in PTSD Management - In times of struggle, having a strong support system can make a huge difference. Connecting with friends and family members, participating in support groups, or even talking to a trusted individual about your experiences can be incredibly healing. You don’t have to go through this alone.

At Frinz Care, we understand the importance of comprehensive care in managing PTSD. We’re here to support you every step of the way, providing a partnership in reclaiming a fulfilling and meaningful life.

The Role Of Faith In Online Christian Counseling - Are you feeling overwhelmed by life’s challenges and searching for support and guidance that aligns with your Christian faith? Perhaps you’ve been considering therapy but are hesitant about fitting in-person sessions into your busy schedule. If so, online christian counseling could be the solution you’re looking for. At Frinz Care, we understand the importance of faith in wellness and healing, and we are committed to providing a space that seamlessly integrates both.

Christian counseling online offers the same professional mental health support as traditional counseling but incorporates spiritual principles and Christian values into the therapy process. This faith-based approach uniquely addresses the physical, emotional, and spiritual aspects of a person’s well-being, offering a holistic solution to the challenges one may face. Additionally, this type of counseling provides the convenience of teletherapy, enabling you to attend to your mental health wherever you feel comfortable and safe.

Key Benefits of Christian Counselling Online:

Efficiency: No travel time, manage sessions around your schedule.

Comfort: Optional to have sessions in a space where you feel most comfortable and safe.

Privacy: Avoid disclosing attending therapy to others if desired.

Access: Removes geographical location as a barrier to access services.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will you share or sell my personal information?

We will not share or sell your personal information to any 3rd party that is not necessary to provide you care. Your personal information is used only to manage your therapy and coaching treatment plans. It will not be shared unless you authorize us to do so in writing, or in the event we receive a subpoena by the court of law requiring the release, and we will only release the necessary information required.

What is a life-coach?

Our coaches have a bachelors, masters, or Ph.D. college degree and between 5 and 30 years of experience coaching teens, young adults, or families. They have completed a life-coaching certification program through an International Coaching Federation (ICF) accredited school (see coachingfederation.org for more information on ICF’s code of ethics and standards of practice). They have experience working with teens, young adults, and families.

How do I get assigned a counselor or coach?

You can schedule a free consultation to discuss your needs and our staff will give you counseling and coach options that will best meet your needs. You can schedule a consultation directly on our website.
