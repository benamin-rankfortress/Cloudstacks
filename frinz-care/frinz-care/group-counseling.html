Understanding Christian Counseling Online - Defining Christian Counseling:

Christian counseling, also known as faith-based counseling, is a therapeutic approach that combines psychological methods with spiritual resources to address mental health issues. The goal is not only to help individuals overcome their challenges but also to strengthen their relationship with God. The core principles of hope and healing through faith are central to this form of therapy.

At Frinz Care, we understand that each individual is unique, and thus our Christian counseling approach is tailored to meet the specific needs and goals of every client. This could involve discussing your Christian identity, the teachings of the Bible related to your current struggles, and guiding you on how to align your coping strategies with your faith.

The Role of Faith in Christian Counseling - Faith plays a critical role in Christian counseling. It serves as a powerful resource for individuals to draw strength and find hope during their trials. Christian counselors integrate prayer and scripture into the sessions, allowing you to explore the spiritual aspects of your life and how they relate to your overall well-being.

We at Frinz Care are committed to providing a compassionate, faith-based approach to support you on your therapeutic journey. We believe in integrating the teachings and principles of Christianity into our sessions, allowing you to explore the spiritual aspects of your life and how they relate to your overall well-being. This way, we help you leverage your faith to overcome your struggles and achieve personal growth.

The Difference Between Christian Counseling and Other Forms of Therapy - While other forms of therapy focus primarily on psychological theories and techniques, Christian counseling integrates the principles and teachings of Christianity. It encompasses a holistic approach to therapy, addressing not just the mental and emotional needs of the client, but also their spiritual well-being.

In Christian counseling, the Bible serves as a primary source of wisdom and guidance. The counselor uses scriptural truths to address the client’s struggles and guide them towards healing and growth. Thus, the difference lies in the perspective – Christian counseling views life and its challenges through the lens of faith.

What is a mental health counselor?

Our counselors have completed a master’s degree or Ph.D. from an accredited mental health counseling college program. They are licensed to work in the state as a marriage and family therapist (LMFT), licensed clinical or social worker (LCSW / LMSW), or licensed professional counselor (LPC), and have between 5 and 30+ years of counseling experience. They have experience working with teens, young adults, and families. They can treat a wide range of mental health issues, but also specialize in a few areas depending on their experience and interests.

How is coaching different from counseling?

Coaching is a form of strengths-based support for persons dealing with a broad range of challenges from living with serious mental illness (e.g., bipolar disorder) to developing good life choices. Coaches do not diagnose disorders and the focus of treatment starts with the current conditions, symptoms, and recent events. Coaches help the client develop plans for future success dealing with their issues; while counseling may dive deep into the client’s history to understand the root causes of the underlying issues, diagnose disorders, and use some different techniques to treat clients.
