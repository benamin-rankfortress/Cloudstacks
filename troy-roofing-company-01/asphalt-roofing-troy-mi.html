*STYLE

The style of your roof should compliment the style of your home. If you have a traditional home, a shingled roof would be a good option. For a more modern home, a metal roof might be the way to go. There are also many eco-friendly roofing options available if you’re looking to be more environmentally conscious.

*COST

The cost of your roof will vary depending on the materials you choose and the size of your home. It’s important to get several estimates from different roofing contractors before making a final decision. Be sure to compare the cost of materials and labor so you can get the best deal.

*DURABILITY

You want your roof to last for many years, so it’s important to choose materials that are durable and will stand up to the elements. Metal roofs are a good option because they’re very durable and can last for decades. Shingled roofs are also a good choice, but they may need to be replaced more often than a metal roof.

*MAINTENANCE

You should also consider the maintenance required for your roof. Some materials, like metal, require very little maintenance. But others, like shingles, need to be replaced or repaired more often. Be sure to ask your roofing contractor about the maintenance required for the material you choose.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I replace or repair my roof?

If your roof has a leak or other minor damages, it can be fixed and you won’t need to replace the entire roof for that. Cracked or missing shingles can be easily repaired and save you a lot of money. But if your roofing system is really old and there are multiple leaks, it’s best to get the entire roof replaced.

What will be the cost of repairing the roof?

It mainly depends on the type of roofing shingle you’re using on your roofing system and the state of your roofing materials. We can always tell you how much it’d cost you after inspecting your roof firsthand.

How long does it take to replace the roofing system?

The average time it takes to completely replace an asphalt roofing system is up to 4 days. However every property and the situation is unique and we have been able to finish projects in a day or two many times in our experience.

How long will my roof last?

That depends on a lot of different factors including the roofing material used and weather conditions throughout the years. We provide enhanced warranties with our roofing services and the roofs do last longer without even needing the warranties. But if we speak on average, the longevity of most roofs is around 15 to 25 years. Metal roofs last even longer than that and that’s why they cost more than other roofing materials.
