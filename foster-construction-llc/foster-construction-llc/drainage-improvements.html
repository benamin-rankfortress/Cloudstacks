How Do I Waterproof My Block Foundation From the Outside?

Assess the Foundation Before you begin waterproofing your block foundation, it's essential to inspect it thoroughly. Look for any visible cracks, gaps, or damaged mortar joints that could serve as potential entry points for water. Take note of problem areas, as addressing them during the waterproofing process will enhance the effectiveness of your efforts.

Clean and Prepare the Surface A clean and well-prepared surface is essential for proper adhesion of waterproofing materials. Use a wire brush or power washer to remove dirt, debris, and loose mortar from the foundation's surface. Make sure the area is completely dry before proceeding to the next step.

Repair Cracks and Mortar Joints Patch up any visible cracks or gaps in the foundation using hydraulic cement or an appropriate concrete patching compound. Pay close attention to corners and intersections, as these areas are particularly vulnerable to water intrusion. Addressing these issues will prevent water from seeping into the foundation later on.

Apply Waterproofing Membrane Waterproofing membranes are an integral part of the exterior foundation waterproofing process. Choose a high-quality, rubberized asphalt membrane designed explicitly for below-grade applications. Roll or brush the membrane onto the prepared surface, ensuring complete coverage.

Install Drainage System An effective drainage system is critical for diverting water away from your foundation. Consider installing perforated drain pipes, also known as weeping tiles, along the exterior perimeter of your foundation. These pipes should be placed in a gravel bed and sloped to direct water away from the foundation towards a proper drainage outlet.

Backfill Properly Once the waterproofing membrane and drainage system are in place, carefully backfill the excavated area around the foundation. Use clean, fill dirt, and avoid using sharp rocks or debris that could damage the waterproofing layer. Ensure the soil slopes away from the foundation to promote water runoff further.

Maintain Landscaping After waterproofing, it's essential to maintain the landscaping around your home. Trim bushes, trees, and shrubs away from the foundation to prevent them from rubbing against the waterproofing membrane or causing damage to the drainage system. Keep gutters and downspouts clean and debris-free to ensure proper water flow from the house.

Waterproofing a block foundation from the exterior is vital to saving your home from severe water-related problems in the long run. By understanding the causes of water infiltration and implementing proper waterproofing techniques, you can protect your foundation and, in turn, your entire home. Regular maintenance and proactive measures will contribute to a dry and secure living space, providing peace of mind and a solid foundation for many years. Remember, investing in waterproofing today can save you from extensive repairs tomorrow. So, take the time and effort to safeguard your home's foundation—it's well worth it!

Foster Construction's team of skilled engineers and technicians are well-versed in identifying these imperceptible deteriorations, preventing them from escalating into major concerns.
