Identifying signs of water damage - Before you start the waterproofing process, it's essential to inspect your block foundation for signs of water damage. Look out for the following indicators:

Damp or musty odors in the basement or crawl space.

Efflorescence: White, chalky substance on the walls, indicating salt deposits left behind by evaporating water.

Cracks in the walls, especially if they are leaking or show signs of water penetration.

Mold or mildew growth on walls or surfaces.

Staining or discoloration on the walls. How exterior waterproofing works. Exterior waterproofing of a block foundation typically involves the following steps:

Excavation: The area around the foundation is excavated to expose the exterior walls completely.

Cleaning and repairing: The walls are cleaned of any dirt, debris, or loose material. Cracks and gaps are repaired to ensure a smooth surface.

Application of waterproofing materials: A waterproofing membrane or coating is applied to the exterior walls. This barrier prevents water from seeping into the foundation, allowing any trapped moisture to escape.

Drainage system: A drainage system, such as perforated pipes or a French drain, redirects water away from the foundation.

Backfilling: Once the waterproofing is in place, the excavated area is backfilled with gravel or soil, ensuring proper drainage from the foundation.

Exterior Block Foundation Waterproofing Methods

Excavation and Drainage: The first step in waterproofing a block foundation from the exterior is to excavate the area around the foundation. This allows access to the foundation walls and footing, making identifying any cracks, defects, or weak points easier. Proper drainage is also crucial for redirecting water away from the foundation. Install a perforated pipe, a weeping tile, alongside the footing to collect and direct groundwater away from the foundation.

Waterproofing Membranes: Waterproofing membranes are an effective barrier against moisture and are commonly used in exterior foundation waterproofing. These membranes are typically made from asphalt-modified polyurethane, rubberized asphalt, or other synthetic materials. The membrane is applied to the foundation walls, creating a seamless and impenetrable barrier that prevents water from seeping into the block walls.

Exterior Sealants and Coatings: Exterior sealants and coatings are complementary methods used in conjunction with waterproofing membranes. These products add an extra layer of protection against water intrusion and can also provide resistance to environmental factors such as UV rays and soil erosion. Acrylic-based sealants and coatings are commonly used for their durability and flexibility.

Step-by-Step Waterproofing Process

Start with a Clean Surface: Before implementing any waterproofing method, clean the exterior surface of the block foundation to ensure optimal adhesion.

Address Existing Cracks: Seal any visible cracks in the blocks with an appropriate sealant to prevent water from entering through these openings.
