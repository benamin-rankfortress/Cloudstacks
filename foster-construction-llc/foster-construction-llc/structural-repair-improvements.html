How To Waterproof A Block Foundation From Exterior - Protecting your home's foundation from water damage is crucial to ensure its longevity and structural integrity. Block foundations are commonly found in many homes among the various types of foundations. Waterproofing a block foundation from the exterior can be challenging yet essential to prevent water infiltration and potential structural issues. In this blog, we will explore the steps and techniques on how to waterproof a block foundation from exterior, safeguarding your home from the damaging effects of water.

Importance of waterproofing block foundations - Block foundations, commonly made of concrete or cinder blocks, are susceptible to water intrusion due to their porous nature. When water seeps into the foundation, it can cause a range of problems, such as mold growth, deterioration of the blocks, and even compromising the structural integrity of the building. Waterproofing your block foundation is essential to prevent these issues and safeguard your investment.

Why Waterproof Your Block Foundation Wall? - Keeping your home's foundation dry is essential to avoid potential moisture-related issues. However, traditional foundation vents lack motors and often struggle to handle excess moisture in unfavorable conditions. This is particularly problematic when block foundation walls allow water into the crawl space.

Block foundation walls are naturally porous, designed to absorb moisture from the mortar. Unfortunately, this also means they can absorb groundwater, which can then reach the framing of your home. It's crucial to waterproof the block foundation and install a vapor barrier to prevent such water absorption. In some cases, adding a french drain can be beneficial. These measures prevent exterior moisture from entering your crawl space or basement through the block walls.

What is Block Foundation Waterproofing - Foundation waterproofing creates a barrier between the foundation and groundwater to prevent water from entering the structure. When it comes to blocking foundations, this process is typically done from the exterior, as it provides a more comprehensive and long-lasting solution compared to interior waterproofing methods.

Causes of water intrusion in block foundations - Before moving forward with waterproofing, it's crucial to understand the primary causes of water intrusion. Some common reasons include:

Poor drainage: Inadequate grading around the foundation can lead to water pooling against the walls, increasing the chances of seepage.

Hydrostatic pressure: Water-saturated soil surrounding the foundation can exert pressure on the walls, forcing water to penetrate through cracks and gaps.

Cracks and gaps: As block foundations age, they can develop cracks and gaps, providing an entry point for water.

Absence of proper waterproofing: Some older buildings may need proper waterproofing measures, making them vulnerable to water damage.
