Healing Past Traumas: Effective Therapy Options for Men

We’re all subject to pain and suffering. Sometimes life hands us moments that leave us feeling shattered and damaged, unable to pick up the pieces on our own. Men, particularly, may find themselves struggling with unresolved issues due to societal pressures and stereotypes that dictate how they should process pain, specifically trauma. This problem often gets overlooked due to the widespread preconception that men should be stoic and invincible – immune to the scarring effects of past traumas. But that’s far from true.

Here at Inner Healing Therapy, we find it crucial to dispel this harmful myth. We recognize that men, like anyone else, can suffer from past traumas, and they need effective therapy for healing. Understanding that addressing this issue isn’t a sign of weakness, but an act of courage and strength is the first step in finding the right healing path.

Men who wrestle with past traumas often exhibit signs such as heightened levels of aggression, difficulty regulating emotions and unhealthy coping mechanisms such as substance abuse. Society may try to trivialize these behaviors, but we understand that these are cries for help masked by the ill-fated need to uphold a false façade of strength.

At ‘Inner Healing Therapy,’ our aim is to highlight the importance of effective therapy for healing past traumas in men, to aid those who silently bear the weight of their untold stories.

FIVE EFFECTIVE THERAPY OPTIONS FOR HEALING PAST TRAUMAS IN MEN

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Focuses on changing negative thought patterns that result from trauma, promoting healthier responses.

Eye-Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR): Helps to process and restructure traumatic memories to reduce their impact.

Prolonged Exposure Therapy (PE): Encourages recollection and engagement with traumatic experiences to diminish their influence.

Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT): Specifically adapted for men suffering from post-traumatic stress disorders.

Holistic Methods (Yoga, Meditation, Mindfulness): Complementing traditional therapies, these methods promote emotional regulation and self-awareness.

How much does therapy cost?

What forms of payment do you accept?

Schedule a free 15-minute consultation to discuss what you’re looking for in therapy and find out if we can help.

Do you only accept male clients?

 

No. Although our practice specializes in men’s mental health, we work with men and women. 

What is your cancellation policy?

 

If you need to reschedule or cancel your appointment, please provide at least 24 business hours’ notice. Business hours are Monday-Friday 9am-6pm.  Sessions canceled less than 24 business hours before the appointment time will be charged the full session rate on the credit card on file.

What are my rights under the No Surprises Act?

 

Under federal law, health care providers must provide patients who don’t have health insurance or are not using insurance with an estimate of their bill for health care services before those services are provided. This estimate is called a Good Faith Estimate.

  • You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate for the total expected cost of services upon request or when scheduling services. 
  • You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate in a way that is accessible to you. 
  • You have the right to have your provider explain the estimate to you upon request.
  • You have the right to dispute a bill for services that is at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate. 

For questions or more information about your right to a Good Faith Estimate, visit www.cms.gov/nosurprises/consumers or call 1- 800-985-3059.
