The Unique Approach of Resilient Mind Counseling to BIPOC Mental Health - Resilient Mind Counseling takes an anti-racist approach to therapy, making it a safe space for individuals who might otherwise feel marginalized or misunderstood in traditional therapy settings. The multicultural counselors at Resilient Mind Counseling understand the unique challenges faced by the BIPOC community and provide a supportive atmosphere where patients are encouraged to explore the root causes of their struggles.

This patient-centered approach helps individuals navigate their personal experiences and societal pressures in a way that fosters healing and growth. Each therapeutic journey at Resilient Mind Counseling is tailored to the individual’s needs, recognizing the importance of their cultural, racial, and ethnic identity.

The Range of Services Offered by Resilient Mind Counseling - Versatility is a prominent feature of Resilient Mind Counseling. They provide a wide range of services, including therapy for anxiety, depression, trauma, mood disorders, PTSD, and more. The therapists at Resilient Mind Counseling also specialize in Neurodivergent therapy and LGBTQIA+ therapy.

In addition, the center offers couples and family counseling, which can be beneficial in addressing familial or relationship-related stressors that may impact mental health. For those who may need medication as part of their treatment plan, Resilient Mind Counseling also has a provider who prescribes psychiatric medication.

Be BOLD Psychology and Consulting: Culturally Aware Therapy and Relationship Support - Be BOLD Psychology and Consulting is another noteworthy practice in North Carolina that offers culturally aware therapy and relationship support. This practice is led by a diverse team of professionals who are committed to providing culturally aware therapy and relationship support for BIPOC clients. Their services include group therapy, couples, relationship, and family therapy, as well as therapy for chronic pain, illness, and caregiver fatigue.

Be BOLD emphasizes the importance of therapy sessions that are driven by clients, encouraging open dialogue and accountability, recognizing that the client is the expert of their own life. The benefit of such an approach is that it allows BIPOC individuals to feel seen, heard, and understood in their experiences.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my therapist is the right one for me?

Take your time to read through this website. If you sense that we may be a good fit based on what you are reading, you can call us.

During a free phone consultation, you can ask one of our therapists about how they have helped other people like you before, ask how they work as a therapist, or any other questions you may have that are specific-to-you. You’ll also be able to tell them what’s up, and you and one of our therapists can decide together how they can help.

It will usually take a couple of sessions to feel comfortable and develop a relationship. During the sessions, you’ll be able to understand how comfortable you feel in sessions. Our approach is not going to work for everyone. What’s most important is that you find someone who you feel is the right fit for you. We can explore that together.

Does what we talk about in therapy remain confidential?

You might already know that you want to share sensitive information about your loved ones and you don’t want them to find out what you’ve said. So we want you to know that unless there is an imminent danger to yourself or someone else, everything you share is completely confidential.

There is nothing more important than your privacy.

You can rest assured that you can talk about anything without fear of that information getting out.

If you have specific questions about how confidentiality works, you can ask during your free phone consultation or anytime during your work with your therapist.

I’m nervous about starting therapy. Is that normal?

Therapy can be vulnerable and new, so it’s natural to be nervous. It can be scary to think of opening up or crying in front of someone new, perhaps fearing that it will be awkward and uncomfortable. The fear around that can feel overwhelming. Getting the counseling we need can be challenging. And although it’s normal to be nervous, that doesn’t make it feel great. It takes courage to continue getting the help you need.

Therapy is a space where you can be yourself and feel safe from judgment, including feeling nervous. We’re committed to finding a way to care for your nervousness and ensure sessions are as comfortable as possible.

We’re here if you want to talk about it during a free phone consultation.
