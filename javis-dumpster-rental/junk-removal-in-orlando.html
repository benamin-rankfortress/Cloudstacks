Frequently Asked Questions

What’s the difference between a roll off container and a dumpster?

Residential dumpster – a standard dumpster usually located in an area of a business or complex for use of the general waste that is produced by those locations. Normally this refuse only consists of household and office waste. These dumpsters are handled on a regular, pre-set schedule by the local waste management service. Dumpsters are emptied into a large compactor and remain as a permanent fixture in the area they are located in.
Roll off containers – designed for construction and demolition clean up. It is important to note that some items are prohibited to be placed in these containers depending on the hauler and local regulations. Roll-off containers are long rectangular dumpsters that typically range in capacity from 10 cu. yds. to 30 cu. yds. When full, a roll off is removed from an area using a special truck designed to carry it from the fill location to the dump site.

How far in advance do I need to order a dumpster rental?

Please order your dumpster 48 hours in advance if possible. However, in certain situations, your Dumpster Rental representative may be able to deliver the container sooner.

How long can I keep the dumpster?

You can keep the dumpster for the entire length of the contract. Typical rental periods are 7-14 days. However, we are very service oriented and we have the flexibility to work with you if it looks like your project may last longer than the agreed upon contract period. Please be aware that additional rental fees may be added to your original invoice. So please call us before the end of the rental agreement and our sales staff will work with you.

What if I need an additional haul?

When you order your dumpster, your Dumpster Rental representative will consult with you to find out the size of your project and schedule the correct amount of hauls needed. We want you to be completely satisfied with your dumpster service so we will schedule any additional hauls, if needed.
