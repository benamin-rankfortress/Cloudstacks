Understanding Addiction Recovery Programs - Addiction recovery programs are structured interventions aimed at helping individuals overcome substance use disorders and regain control of their lives. The primary purpose of these programs is to provide support, guidance, and treatment strategies to individuals struggling with addiction, and to equip them with the necessary skills to maintain long-term sobriety.

Types of Recovery Programs:

Inpatient Programs - Inpatient programs offer a structured environment where individuals stay at a facility for a certain period, usually 30, 60, or 90 days. These programs provide intensive treatment and round-the-clock medical supervision.

Outpatient Programs - Outpatient programs allow individuals to live at home and continue their daily responsibilities while attending treatment sessions. We at Core Recovery offer outpatient programs that are flexible and can be tailored to fit your schedule.

12-Step Programs - 12-Step programs, like Alcoholics Anonymous and Narcotics Anonymous, are community-based programs that offer peer support and a structured approach to recovery, based on a series of steps.

Non-12-Step Programs - Non-12-Step programs provide alternative approaches to recovery, often incorporating evidence-based therapies and may focus on self-reliance rather than reliance on a higher power.

Holistic Recovery Programs - Holistic recovery programs aim to treat the whole person, addressing the physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual aspects of addiction. They often incorporate practices like yoga, meditation, and nutrition counseling.

Components of Effective Recovery Programs

Individualized Treatment Plans - An effective recovery program acknowledges the fact that each individual’s journey to recovery is unique. At Core Recovery, we create individualized treatment plans based on an in-depth assessment of your needs, diagnosis, and recovery progress.

Medical Detoxification - Medical detoxification is often the first step in a recovery program, helping individuals safely withdraw from the substances they are addicted to under medical supervision.

Therapy and Counseling - Various forms of therapy and counseling are used in recovery programs, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), and family counseling. These therapies aim to address the underlying causes of addiction and develop healthier coping mechanisms.

Aftercare and Relapse Prevention - Aftercare and relapse prevention strategies are crucial components of recovery programs, ensuring individuals have the support and skills needed to maintain sobriety after completing treatment. This could include ongoing therapy, support group meetings, and regular check-ups with a recovery specialist.

Understanding the different types of addiction recovery programs available in Phoenix can help you or your loved one make an informed decision about the best path to recovery. At Core Recovery, we are committed to helping individuals navigate their recovery journey with compassion and expertise.

The Role of Social Skills in Recovery - Recovery from addiction isn’t just about overcoming physical dependencies; it also involves building and strengthening social skills that can foster resilience and support long-lasting recovery. At Core Recovery, we understand the importance of these skills and incorporate them into our individualized treatment plans for our clients in Phoenix.

Importance of Communication and Interpersonal Skills - Effective communication and strong interpersonal skills are critical in addiction recovery. They enable individuals to express their thoughts, feelings, and needs clearly and constructively, facilitating better relationships with therapists, family members, and peers in recovery groups. These skills also help individuals to seek and receive help when needed, and to navigate social situations without resorting to substance use.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long are Core Recovery's IOP and PHP programs?

We create an individual treatment plan for each patient, so the length of the program is tailored based on our patient’s clinical assessment and designed to create a transition to a happier and more rewarding life. On average our patients’ program at Core Recovery between three and six months.

Can I take FLMA or Short-Term Disability while in the program?

It is very common for patients to apply for FMLA or Short-Term Disability while in the program. Often this is dependent on your level of care, which is determined during your initial intake appointment. Core’s PHP patients participate in the program for a minimum of 20 hours a week. The IOP requires a minimum of 9 hours and up to 19 hours a week. As part of your program at Core you will be expected to participate in groups, individual counseling, family systems counseling and psychiatric/medication management appointments.

Can I see a counselor at Core without doing groups?

Groups are the foundation of our program and are required at all levels of care. The number of groups will be prescribed to you at your initial mental health intake appointment. Once you are a patient here at Core you may meet with one of our clinicians for a weekly 60-minute individual counseling, 60-minute family systems counseling and your 30-minute psychiatric/medication appointment.
